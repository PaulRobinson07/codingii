Terry A. Davis

Life Summary

Terry A. Davis, born Terrence Andrew Davis on December 15,1969, was considered a genius level electrical engineer, computer programmer, and artist. He is the sole founder, creator, and user of his own operating system named Temple OS. Davis experienced numerous manic episodes, leading to hospitalization and being diagnosed with bipolar disorder. He was homeless for the eigth months leading up to his death and followers of his youtube channel tried desperately to help him get off the streets, but he died on August 11, 2018 after getting hit by a train at the age of 48.

Early Years

Davis grew up Catholic and often moved throughout the country. In elementary school, his parents bought him an Apple II which allowed him to gain familiarity with computers. As a teen he bought a Commodore 64 and started programming in assembly. He then earned his Masters in Electrical Engineering from ASU in 1994 and has been quoted as saying, "Everybody knows electrical (engineering) is higher in the engineering pecking order than [computer systems] because it requires real math."

Post Graduation

Following graduation, Davis worked at Tickmaster with their ticket machines. Davis became an athiest, but after a manic episode, he converted to Christianity. He then went on to create Temple OS which had it's own programming language, text editor, compiler, and kernel with over 120,000 lines of code. He thought that he was told by God to design the operating system and he started posting about it online. Since he started working on Temple OS in the early 2000s, he had less hospital visits, but his condition was still deterioating

Later Years

Davis spent a large majority of his time talking on online forums about his revelations, racist viewpoints, and hate speach which led him to get banned time and time again. This same thing happened on Youtube, leading his Youtube channel getting taken down multiple times. Alongside this he was under the assumption that he was being watched by the CIA or aliens. Davis was incarcerated numerous times before his death and also became homeless. He passed on August 11, 2018 after getting hit by a train. Investigators were unable to tell if it was or wasn't a suicide
